Current Focus

A few months ago, a local gym owner told me he'd tried and failed to get custom software built. My "Traditional Product" brain immediately kicked in: I can help you define the product and make sure a developer builds it right.

Except, I didn't need to wait. I started experimenting—first with Lovable, and then moving locally with Claude Code to build and deploy.

Six weeks later, I realized I could have built the production MVP myself.

The developer role hasn't disappeared; they've just moved from the entry point to the scale-up partner for a product that's already working. But this only works if the Product thinking is rigorous enough to drive the build—not just hand it off.

My work now centers on AI Governance: designing the frameworks that allow Product teams to work effectively with AI. It's about ensuring that the Product discipline remains the anchor as engineering velocity accelerates.

Methodology

AI doesn't wait. Neither does a poorly scoped feature.

Push left means the direction is locked before anyone touches a keyboard—using AI-driven artifacts as engineering inputs and stakeholder alignment tools.

Push right means being in the room during the build, proving the intent through working software rather than just explaining it in a brief.

AI moves fast. The Product discipline is what makes that speed count.
