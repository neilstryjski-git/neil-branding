## **The Gym Owner Story [COMMITTED]**

A few months ago my gym owner told me he'd tried and failed to get custom software built for his business. My instinct was immediate and completely automatic: *help him define the product and make sure a developer builds it right.*

Neither of us questioned it. Of course you need a developer to build software.

Six weeks later, I would have built it myself.

Not handed it off. Not found a developer. Built it — concept to active users — myself. That's what building Itinerary Wizard taught me. I started in Lovable, hit its limits, moved the whole thing to my local machine, and kept going until real users were using it.

The developer role didn't disappear. It moved. From *entry point* to *scale-up partner* for a product that's already in the hands of real users.

That's a fundamentally different build cycle. And it only works if the Product thinking is rigorous enough to drive the build — not just hand it off.

## **The Outcome Engine: Realizing the AI Gain [DRAFT]**

**The Thesis** Most organizations invest in AI tools expecting immediate productivity leaps, but the gains often stall. The bottleneck is often a discovery process that hasn't evolved and a Product function that hasn't yet integrated with this new technical reality. I help organizations bridge this gap by architecting a more rigorous Product-to-Engineering workflow.

**The Strategy**

* **Pushing the Boundary Left:** I move Product thinking deeper into the technical architecture. By engaging with the system design early, I ensure the foundation is built to handle AI-driven velocity without structural debt.  
* **Pushing the Boundary Right:** I guide the shift from traditional documentation to "Working Proofs." This allows organizations to validate high-stakes ideas in a production-ready environment before committing full resources to scale them.  
* **Documentation as a Result:** In this model, documentation isn't a prerequisite for starting; it is a high-fidelity result of the build. Better specs and clearer architectural records are generated as a byproduct of a working system.

**The Strategic Objective** My goal is to help organizations expand the Product footprint in both directions, ensuring the transition to technical scaling is built on a proven foundation rather than a theoretical one. This approach is highly scalable, allowing the depth of the initial build to be calibrated based on the specific segment and complexity of the organization. By the time a project reaches the scale-up phase, engineering is focusing on hardening and growth because the strategic and technical translation tax has already been paid.

## **Push Left / Push Right — The New PM Boundary [COMMITTED]**

The PM's old boundary was defined by explaining the idea. The new boundary is defined by proving it.

**The Traditional Model:** Product work sits primarily in the discovery phase. While high-quality Product Management was deeply integrated through Three Amigo meetings, technical consultations, and formal acceptance cycles, these remained points of alignment rather than execution. The process outputted artifacts upward for a handoff, after which Engineering would begin the build.

**The Agentic Model:** Product work spans the full width of the cycle. The collaboration of the Three Amigos doesn't end at a meeting; it is baked into the AI guardrails from day one. Engineering joins earlier and goes deeper, focusing on scaling a product that might already have active user validation. In this model, Acceptance isn't a final meeting; it is a continuous state of the working build.

## **The Product Trio Agentic Framework [DRAFT]**

I implement a framework designed to anchor AI execution and eliminate the semantic noise that occurs in long-context AI sessions:

* **The Mind (High-Entropy):** A fluid environment for the Product Trio to debate, experiment, and pivot without the friction of formal documentation.  
* **The Bedrock:** An immutable source of truth that serves as the governor for execution. Rather than a static archive, this documentation is a high-density input into the build. It transforms intent into the Charter and Specs that ensure coding agents build exactly what was intended.  
* **The Handshake:** The process of converting messy "Mind" intent into clean context for coding agents. This ensures the strategic and technical translation tax is paid upfront before a single line of code is scaled.
